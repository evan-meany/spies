# **Spies Card Game**

## **Overview**
- The game is played with a standard 52 playing card deck
- 2 players
- The game consists of 3 rounds
- The player who wins more rounds wins the game
- Rounds and the game may end in a tie
- Hands carry over between rounds
- SPADES ARE SPIES

# **The Board**
The board is divided into two halves:
* **Player 1’s side**
* **Player 2’s side**

Each side has **three rows**:
* Clubs Row
* Hearts Row
* Diamonds Row

The physical order of the rows does not matter, but it should remain **consistent for both players**.

# **The Cards**
### **Values**
| Card | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Ace | 3 points |
| 10 – King | 2 points |
| 3 – 9 | 1 point |
| 2 | 1 point (special ability: **Double Agent**) |
### **Clubs, Hearts, Diamonds**
These cards must be played on **your side of the board** in their **matching suit row**.
### **Spades (Spies)**
Spades are used to **sabotage opponent cards**.
#### Playing Spies
- A Spy is placed **on top of an opponent’s card**
- The Spy **subtracts its value** from that card
- Multiple Spies may be placed on the same card
- A card’s value **cannot go below 0**
#### Example
- Opponent plays **Jack of Hearts (2 points)**
- You play **6♠ (1 point)** on top of it.
- The Jack is now worth **1 point**.
- Each Spy affects **only the card it is placed on**.
#### Trading Spies
- Instead of playing a Spy, you may **trade it in**.
- Discard the Spy
- Draw **one card from the deck**
- You may **trade only one Spy per turn**.
### **Double Agents (2s)**
All **2s** are **Double Agents** and have special abilities.
#### **2 of Clubs, Hearts, Diamonds**
When played, you may choose one of two actions:
1. Replace
    - Replace an opponent’s card **of the same suit**.
    - The **2** takes that card’s place
    - The replaced card moves to **your side of the board** in the correct row
    - Scoring:
        - Opponent receives **1 point** for the 2
        - You receive the **value of the replaced card**
2. Play Normally
    - Place the 2 on **your side** in its suit row.
    - It is worth **1 point**.
#### **2 of Spades**
- The **2♠** is a special Spy.
- You may choose one of two actions:
1. Replace an Opponent Spy
    - Replace an opponent’s **played Spy**
    - The replaced Spy is immediately **played onto an opponent card**
    - The 2♠ remains and subtracts **1 point** from the card it sits on
2. Play as a Spy
    - Place it on an opponent card like a normal Spy.
    - It subtracts **1 point**.

# **Game Play**
## Game Setup
- Players sit **opposite each other**.
- Deal **15 cards** to each player.
- Players may organize their hands.
- Each player **discards 5 cards** face down (ending with **10 cards**).
- Combine the **discarded cards with the remaining deck**, shuffle, and place it between the players.

## Round Play
### Round Start
- Each round begins by both players flipping **one card from the deck face up**.
- The player with the **higher value card goes first** (Aces are high).
- Each player **adds their revealed card to their hand**.
### Player Turn
On their turn, a player may choose one of the following:
- **Play a card** from their hand
- **Pass**
#### Playing a Card
- A player places a card from their hand onto the board.
- If the card is a **Club, Heart, or Diamond**, it must be placed on the player's side in the **matching suit row**.
- If the card is a **Spade (Spy)**, it must be placed on **an opponent's card** on the opponent's side of the board.
#### Passing
- When a player passes, they may **no longer play cards during that round**.
- After a player passes, the opponent may continue taking turns until they also pass.
- If a player has **no legal moves**, they **automatically pass**.

### Round End
- The round ends once **both players have passed**.

### Determining the Round Winner
- Each row is evaluated separately.
- The player with the **higher total card value** in a row **wins that row**.
- If both players have **equal points**, the row is **tied**.
- If **neither player has any points** in a row, the row is also **tied**.
- Tied rows **do not count toward either player**.
The player who wins **the most rows** wins the round.
- If both players win the **same number of rows**, the round is a **tie**.

## Determining the Game Winner
- After **3 rounds**, the player who won **the most rounds** wins the game.
- **Tied rounds do not count** toward determining a winner.
- If both players win the **same number of rounds**, the game ends in a **tie**.
- If a player wins the **first two rounds**, the third round may be skipped.
